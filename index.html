What is Occupational therapy?

Occupational therapy is the only profession that helps people across the lifespan to do the things they want and need to do through the therapeutic use of daily activities (occupations). Occupational therapy practitioners enable people of all ages to live life to its fullest by helping them promote health, and prevent—or live better with—injury, illness, or disability.

Who is Audiologist?

An audiologist is a health-care professional specializing in identifying, diagnosing, treating and monitoring disorders of the auditory and vestibular system portions of the ear. Audiologists are trained to diagnose, manage and/or treat hearing or balance problems. They dispense hearing aids and recommend and map cochlear implants. They counsel families through a new diagnosis of hearing loss in infants, and help teach coping and compensation skills to late-deafened adults.

What is Speech therapy?

Speech therapy is a rehabilitative procedure undertaken in order to help the people having any kind of communication disorder and swallowing problems.

Who is Speech Language Pathologist?

A person who specializes in the diagnostic procedures, evaluation techniques, assessment tools and various therapeutic techniques related to speech, language, cognitive- communication, voice, swallowing, fluency and other related disorders.
